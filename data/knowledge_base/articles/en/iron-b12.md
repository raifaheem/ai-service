# Iron and Vitamin B12: The Two Most Common Nutrient Deficiencies

Unexplained fatigue, shortness of breath on mild exertion, pallor, hair loss, tingling in the hands and feet — these symptoms can have many causes, but deficiency of iron or vitamin B12 is among the most common and most overlooked.

## Iron deficiency

Iron is essential for hemoglobin — the protein that carries oxygen. When iron is low, red blood cells can't deliver enough oxygen, and every tissue "runs out of breath".

### Who's at risk

- **Women of reproductive age** — menstrual blood loss is the main cause.
- **Pregnant women** — need up to twice the normal amount.
- **Vegetarians and vegans** — plant iron is absorbed 3× less efficiently.
- **People with chronic GI bleeding** (ulcers, polyps, hemorrhoids).
- **Children during rapid growth**, especially if diet is poor in meat.
- **Elite endurance athletes** — foot-strike hemolysis and GI losses.

### Symptoms

- Fatigue out of proportion to activity.
- Shortness of breath with mild exertion, rapid heartbeat.
- Pallor, especially of inner eyelids and gums.
- Hair loss, brittle nails, sometimes spoon-shaped.
- Unusual cravings for ice, chalk, clay (pica).
- Restless legs at night.

### Testing

- **Ferritin** is the most informative marker of iron stores. Less than 30 ng/ml usually indicates deficiency even if hemoglobin is normal.
- **Complete blood count** — decreased hemoglobin and mean cell volume (MCV) indicate iron-deficiency anemia.
- **Serum iron, TIBC, transferrin saturation** — used as additional markers.

### Dietary sources

- **Heme iron** (from animals, absorbed 20–30%): red meat, liver, fish, poultry.
- **Non-heme iron** (plant, absorbed 5–10%): lentils, beans, spinach, tofu, buckwheat, fortified cereals.

Vitamin C (citrus, peppers, berries) in the same meal improves absorption of non-heme iron 3–4 fold. Coffee, tea, and calcium reduce it — best taken 1–2 hours apart.

### Supplements

Confirmed deficiency is treated with 40–100 mg of elemental iron per day, often with meals to reduce GI side effects. Course usually 3 months with retest. Side effects (nausea, constipation, dark stool) are common but harmless.

Never take iron "prophylactically" without testing — excess iron is pro-oxidant and harmful to the liver and heart.

## Vitamin B12 deficiency

B12 is needed for DNA synthesis, red blood cell formation, and nervous system function. A deficiency develops slowly — stores in the liver last 3–5 years — but once manifest, it can cause neurological damage.

### Who's at risk

- **Vegans and strict vegetarians** — B12 is found almost exclusively in animal products.
- **Older adults** — age-related decline in stomach acid impairs absorption.
- **People on long-term metformin or proton pump inhibitors**.
- **After gastric or ileal surgery** (gastrectomy, Roux-en-Y, Crohn's resection).
- **Autoimmune pernicious anemia** — rare but important cause.

### Symptoms

- Fatigue, weakness.
- **Tingling, numbness** in hands and feet.
- Gait instability, balance problems.
- Memory and concentration problems, depression.
- Smooth, red, "burning" tongue.
- Macrocytic anemia — large red blood cells.

Neurological symptoms can progress even with normal hemoglobin, so B12 should be measured based on symptoms, not just on a CBC result.

### Testing

- **Serum B12** — below 200 pg/ml is clearly deficient; 200–300 is a "gray zone" where additional markers help.
- **Methylmalonic acid, homocysteine** — elevated in true tissue deficiency.

### Dietary sources

- Meat, fish, poultry — naturally rich.
- Eggs, dairy — moderate content.
- Vegans should use fortified foods (plant milks, cereals, nutritional yeast) or B12 supplements.

### Supplements

- Oral B12 (500–1000 mcg per day) is effective in most deficiencies.
- For severe deficiency with neurological symptoms or absorption issues — intramuscular injections are used initially.
- Strict vegans should take B12 supplements regularly regardless of symptoms.

## When to see a doctor

- Fatigue that persists more than a month despite adequate sleep.
- Shortness of breath on mild activity, palpitations.
- Tingling or numbness in the hands and feet.
- Pallor, hair loss, unusual cravings.
- All elderly people and strict vegans should have B12 levels checked periodically.

Deficiencies of iron and B12 are easily diagnosed with inexpensive blood tests and typically respond well to treatment. Don't self-medicate "for energy" without testing — the wrong supplement can mask diagnosis or harm.
