Migraine is a neurological disorder characterized by recurrent headache attacks.
These attacks are often associated with nausea, photophobia, and phonophobia.
Some patients experience aura before the headache begins.
Clinical evaluation should take red-flag symptoms into account, including sudden severe onset,
fever, confusion, focal neurological deficits, and progressive worsening.
Migraine symptoms can overlap with other conditions, so diagnosis should not be made
from a short text description alone.
Rest in a dark room, hydration, and trigger avoidance may help some patients.
